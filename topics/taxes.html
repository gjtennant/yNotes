Taxes should be cut.

Reasons Why

  • Taxes means throwing money at the problem, encouraging waste and fraud.

    Our public school system is a perfect example of the false idea that taxing and spending will solve our problems.  This approach makes things worse, not better.

  • Taxes are theft.

    Taxes are when the government takes your money against your will.

  • Redistribution is morally wrong.

    Taxes are mostly about taking money from those who earn it and giving it to those who don't.  Able-bodied people should earn their own money.

  • There's too much collectivism.

    It's gotten way out of control.  Taxes should only fund what's absolutely necessary, like national defense, police protection, and the highway system.

  • We have perfectly good alternatives to excessive taxation.

    We have local communities, churches, and charities, and the largesse of the rich — like when Carnegie built 2,500 public libraries, or the Gates Foundation today.

  • Taxes undermine self-reliance.

    Arbitrary redistribution sends the wrong message to entrepreneurs and to the lazy alike.  Our system should encourage everyone to work hard, not discourage them.

Reasons Why Not

  • Taxes pay for civilization.

    The free market can increase our standard of living, but it doesn't give us civilization — provide quality of life, dignity, protection of the vulnerable, a sound infrastructure, etc.

  • Taxes are critical to preventing the return of aristocracy.

    The founding fathers worried about some families accumulating so much wealth that they would create a political aristocracy, where wealth and power are passed down from generation to generation.

  • Taxes make everyone pitch in to solve the big problems.

    Anti-tax folks like to say that private giving can solve problems like poverty. But experience shows that charity can't actually solve the big problems.

  • Cutting taxes means destroying the public systems we've built over the years.

    This includes our road system, public schools, social security system, protection of the quality of our air, water, and food supply, protection of the environment, and on and on.
