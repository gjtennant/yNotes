Taxes should be cut.

Reasons Why

  • Taxes means throwing money at the problem, encouraging waste and fraud.

    Our public school system is a perfect example of the false idea that taxing and spending is the solution to our problems. This approach just makes things worse.

  • Taxes undermine self-reliance.

    Arbitrary redistribution sends the wrong message to entrepreneurs and the lazy alike. Our system should encourage everyone to work hard, not discourage them.

  • Redistribution is morally wrong.

    Taxes are mostly about taking money from those who earn it and giving it to those who don't. Able-bodied people should earn their own money.

  • There's too much collectivism.

    It's gotten way out of control. Taxes should only fund what's absolutely necessary, like national defense, police and fire protection, and the highway system.

  • We have perfectly good alternatives to excessive taxation.

    We have local communities, churches, and charities, and the largesse of the rich – like when Carnegie built 2,500 public libraries, or the Gates Foundation today.

  • Taxes are theft.

    Taxes are when the government takes your money against your will.

Reasons Why Not

  • Taxes pay for civilization.

    The free market can increase our standard of living, but it doesn't give us civilization – dignity, protection of the vulnerable, a sound infrastructure, air and water quality, etc.

  • Taxes make everyone pitch in to solve the big problems.

    Anti-tax folks like to say that private giving can solve problems like poverty. But experience shows that charity never solves the big problems.

  • Taxes are critical to preventing the return of aristocracy.

    The Founding Fathers hated the idea of a few families passing down wealth and power from generation to generation.

  • Cutting taxes means destroying the public systems we've built over the years.

    This includes our roads; public schools; social security system; protection of our air, water, food supply, and environment; and on and on.
