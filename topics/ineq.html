We've reached an unacceptable level of economic inequality.

Reasons Why

  • Extreme inequality is bad for democracy.

    Whoever owns the country gets to make the decisions. With extreme inequality only few people own the country. This used to be called an aristocracy.

  • Extreme inequality is bad for the economy.

    Middle-class people spend most of their money, which boosts the economy. Very rich people don't (it's hard to spend millions every month), which keeps money out of the economy.

  • Extreme inequality is the result of a rules changes.

    Very high inequality has come from recent changes in rules — like the tax code. This has allowed people who used to be just successful to become obscenely rich.

Reasons Why Not

  • Freedom leads to inequality.

    Inequality, even great inequality, is the cost of Freedom. And Freedom is our core American value.

  • Inequality promotes individual effort.

    Human nature dictates that we only give our best when we know that there will be winners and losers. Competition is what has made America great.

  • The alternative to inequality is socialism.

    Inequality has its dark side, but the alternative is much worse. It's basically government controlling wealth, either by redistributing it or by getting rid of the free market altogether.
