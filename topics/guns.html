We should be able to regulate guns.

Reasons Why

  • Guns produce a climate of fear.

    The flood of unregulated guns mean that cops now have approach drivers at a traffic stop with drawn guns. Why would we want to go back to the Old West?

  • What's so special about guns?

    Does the Second Amendment cover the right to bear rocket launchers? Hand-grenades? Flame throwers? Suicide vests? Brass knuckles? Tanks? If we can regulate these, we can regulate guns.

  • All rights are subject to regulation.

    We can't yell "fire!" in a crowded theater or call for the violent overthrow of the government or slander people. Why is the Second Amendment different from the others?

  • Each state has different customs and problems.

    Why should New York City and rural Montana be covered by the same gun laws?  This is exactly what States' Rights are for.

  • The Second Amendment has disgusting origins.

    It was insisted on by the Southern states because of their slave rebellion problem, not to protect us from our own government or from criminals.

Reasons Why Not

  • The Second Amendment is pretty clear.

    If we don't like it, then we should amend it.  But for now, as the Supreme Court decided, it gives us the right to bear arms.

  • I have the right to defend myself and my family from crime.

    There are lots of guns and bad people out there, and not enough police.  The government doesn't have the right to take away my right to defend myself.

  • An armed citizenry discourages dictatorships.

    Street protests are a way of pushing back against government power.  An armed citizenry is the last line of defense against tyranny.
