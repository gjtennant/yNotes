We should be able to regulate guns.

Reasons Why

  • Guns create a climate of fear.

    The flood of unregulated guns is a threat to civilized society. Why should our pre-schoolers have to live in fear?

  • One size does not fit all.

    Why should New York City and rural Montana be covered by the same gun laws? Let the states decide what's right for them.

  • What's so special about guns?

    Does the Second Amendment cover the right to bear rocket launchers? Hand-grenades? Flame throwers? Suicide vests? Brass knuckles? Tanks? If we can regulate these, we can regulate guns.

  • All rights are subject to regulation.

    We can't yell "fire!" in a crowded theater or call for the violent overthrow of the government. Why is the Second Amendment different from the others?

  • The Second Amendment has disgusting origins.

    It was insisted on by the Southern states because of their slave rebellion problem, not to protect us from our own government or from criminals.

Reasons Why Not

  • The Second Amendment is crystal clear.

    If we don't like it, then we should amend it. But for now, as the Supreme Court decided, it gives us the right to bear arms.

  • I have a right to defend myself and my family from crime.

    It's a dangerous world, and the government doesn't have the right to take away my ability to defend myself.

  • An armed citizenry discourages dictatorships.

    An armed citizenry is the last line of defense against tyranny.
