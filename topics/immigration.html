We should enforce our immigration laws.

Reasons Why

  • They're fundamental laws.

    What more basic law can a nation have than those that give control over its borders?

    Share
  • We don't actually need more immigrants.

    We don't need to populate empty lands or factories. And we've got plenty of diversity at this point.

    Share
  • Ignoring immigration laws is not civil disobedience.

    Civil rights workers were making a statement of conscience, and willing to go to jail for their beliefs. People who violate our immigration laws are acting out of self-interest.

    Share
  • Uncontrolled immigration is a clear and present danger to our country.

    Overpopulation threatens our environment. Unassimilated populations threaten our institutions, including our schools and language. Poor immigrants lower wages, drain social services, and increase crime.

    Share
  • Illegal immigration leads to exploitation of a vulnerable group.

    US employers take advantage of illegal immigrants. The best way to stop this exploitation is to enforce our immigration laws.

Reasons Why Not

  • Illegal immigrants are a pillar of our economy.

    Multiply 10 million workers by 2,000 hours per year by $5 per hour (or more) that they're underpaid. That adds up to a $100 billion gift to our economy – far more than what they cost us in services.

    Share
  • Most illegal immigrants are Americans in all but name.

    These are folks who've been here most of their lives, are law-abiding, speak English, go to school, and pay taxes.

    Share
  • Immigrants contribute a lot to our society.

    They're incredibly motivated, hard-working, and resourceful individuals. It's what made us great and what will continue to make us great.

    Share
  • We invited them here.

    We ask illegal immigrants to work – in our fields, restaurants, and homes. It seems hypocritical to invite them in and then try to kick them out.

    Share
  • Immigration laws promote exploitation of a vulnerable group.

    The best way to prevent this exploitation is to give illegal immigrants a path to citizenship.

    Share
  • We're all immigrants.

    The vast majority of Americans are immigrants or descended from immigrants. It seems hypocritical to pull up the ladder now that we're safely aboard.

    Share
  • It's inhumane to deport illegal immigrants.

    They don't have a home anywhere else, it breaks up families, and many of these immigrants would be put into very dangerous situations by being deported to their home countries.
