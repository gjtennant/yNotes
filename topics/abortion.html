Abortion should be highly restricted.

Reasons Why

  • It will be a person very soon.

    It doesn't make sense that a fetus a few weeks away from personhood should have no rights. In the case of human beings, shouldn't we err on the side of caution?

    Share
  • The fetus can feel pain even before it becomes a full person.

    Protection from harm should be based on the capacity to suffer. That rule should apply to fetuses as well as animals.

    Share
  • Abortion is not part of God's plan.

    Fertilization is a clear expression of God's intention to produce a baby.

    Share
  • A fetus, even an embryo, has rights as a member of the human species.

    It really doesn't matter whether the fetus is a "person," in the conventional sense of being developed enough to look like a person.

Reasons Why Not

  • We don't know when a fetus becomes a person.

    Therefore abortion, especially early-stage abortion, should be an individual choice – based on a woman's conscience, not on government dictates.

    Share
  • A woman has the right to make choices about her own body.

    Pregnancy is a physiological process. A woman's body determines whether the zygote will implant and whether the fetus will be carried to term.
