Carbon pollution is causing global warming and should be regulated.

Reasons Why

  • The scientific consensus leaves no room for doubt.

    The national academies of science in every major country all agree that carbon pollution is causing global warming and should be reduced immediately.

  • There's a very high risk that we're causing a worldwide catastrophe.

    Would you let your family drink water that 9 out of 10 tests said was poisoned? Would you ridicule scientists who tried to raise the alarm about it?

  • The issue of global warming is like where smoking was 25 years ago.

    Just like the tobacco industry used to do, the fossil fuel industry pays "experts" to muddy the waters – so profits can roll in a little while longer.

  • Even the oil companies are saying global warming is caused by people.

    If the CEOs of Shell Oil and Exxon-Mobil agree with the science – not to mention the Pope and the President of the United States – where does that leave you?

  • The Bible tells us to be concerned about global warming.

    God appointed mankind to be steward of this world (Genesis 1:28). What more important stewardship decision is there than addressing global warming?

  • Carbon pollution is causing acidification of the oceans.

    A more acidic ocean dissolves the calcium shells of plankton, the base of the ocean's entire food chain.

  • It's time to have Congressional hearings about the global warming "hoax."

    Is there a conspiracy by the world's scientists to destroy our economy, or by industry to put civilization at risk for a few extra years of profit?

  • The catastrophe will hit the world's poor first and hardest.

    Allowing hundreds of millions of people to die from extreme weather events caused by our carbon pollution will make us the greatest mass murderers in History.

Reasons Why Not

  • There will be technological fixes to the problem of global warming.

    Research is being conducted into solutions like carbon capture and geo-engineering, as well as alternative energy. We need to let innovation take its course.

  • The people in charge don't seem concerned about global warming.

    If the Cold War were back, our leaders would be making it a priority. In the case of global warming, we hear lip service but no real urgency.

  • Science changes its mind all the time.

    Is fat good or bad for you? What about carbs or salt? Chances are that the alarm over global warming is another hysteria that will fade with time.

  • It's a conspiracy by scientists.

    Mainstream scientists are a tight-knit community controlling grants and publishing. This is the perfect recipe for reinforcing a false consensus on global warming.

  • It's a conspiracy by proponents of World Government.

    Agreeing with the alarmists on global warming means giving more power – perhaps emergency powers – to our government, and even to international organizations like the UN.

  • Some scientists disagree with the consensus.

    Every scientific breakthrough is achieved by scientists who buck the conventional wisdom. The minority of scientists who disagree with the conventional theory of human-caused global warming may well be right.

  • Science doesn't really understand climate change very well.

    This means we shouldn't rush to conclusions and certainly not risk damaging our already fragile economy with overreactions to a problem that may turn out to be nothing.

  • The Bible says that we shouldn't hold Nature higher than God.

    Environmentalism has a tendency to become a form of idolatry, where the "rights" of animals and an inanimate planet are held in higher esteem than God (Romans 1:25).

  • The idea that humans have the power to change Earth's climate is absurd.

    The climate is too big, and too complex, and humans are far too small and insignificant.
