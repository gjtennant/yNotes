We should work to eliminate nuclear weapons.

Reasons Why

  • Nukes are useless against terrorists.

    Say Al-Qaeda got a hold of a bomb and used it against us. What civilian population would we nuke?

  • Nukes are not a useful deterrent against fanatics.

    The idea of deterrence fails completely in the case of a suicide bomber, or a fanatical state. They don't care about the consequences to themselves.

  • Nukes aren't needed even against nuclear states.

    Imagine that Iran or North Korea actually used a nuclear weapon. We could easily obliterate Teheran or Pyongyang with conventional carpet-bombing.

  • Conservative statesmen have recognized that nukes make us less safe.

    George Shultz, Henry Kissinger, and Ronald Reagan have all argued for the gradual elimination of all nuclear weapons.

  • Nuclear exchanges can cause nuclear winters.

    If India and Pakistan had a nuclear exchange, this could cause enough soot to reach the atmosphere to block sunlight and interrupt the food chain for the whole planet.

  • Our nukes encourage others to get them.

    Iran wouldn't push for nukes if the US and Israel didn't have them. And if we didn't have them, we could easily make sure they didn't either.

  • Weak states with nukes are a scary prospect.

    We'll be sorry we didn't push harder to end the nuclear era if Pakistan ever falls to the Taliban – or if terrorists steal or buy nukes from Pakistan's army.

  • Nuclear weapons are designed to kill civilians.

    They can't be targeted against military installations because their impact zone is too big. Killing civilians with weapons of mass destruction is a crime against humanity.

Reasons Why Not

  • Nukes preserve peace.

    We haven't had a world war since the introduction of nuclear weapons. This is because of deterrence – every country is kept in line by the idea of "mutual assured destruction."

  • The cold war might be coming back.

    Russia and China both have been more aggressive of late. This is a bad time to be thinking about getting rid of our nuclear defense.
