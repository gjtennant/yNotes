Right-to-Work laws should be supported.

Right-to-work laws allow people to work in a unionized workplace without joining the union or paying dues to that union.

Reasons Why

  • Right-to-Work laws protect a worker's right to choose.

    Why should a worker be forced to support an organization he/she does not like? Forced unionism is anti-American.

  • Right-to-Work laws create jobs.

    Unions hinder economic growth, and Right-to-Work laws provide a check on union power.

Reasons Why Not

  • The real purpose of Right-to-Work laws is to kill unions.

    If you wanted to kill an insurance company, you'd pass a law saying the company had to cover everyone, but that paying the insurance premiums was optional.

    Email
    Tweet
  • Right-to-Work laws mean union members have to pay for non-union members.

    With Right-to-Work, a union has to negotiate on behalf of all workers, so even the non-union workers benefit from the union – without paying any dues.

    Email
    Tweet
  • Right-to-Work leads to lower wages for workers.

    Workers in Right-to-Work states make about $1,500 less per year than those in non-Right-to-Work states.
