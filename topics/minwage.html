The minimum wage should be enough to live on.

Reasons Why

  • A poverty wage means taxpayers like you have to pay for the food stamps.

    Wouldn't it be simpler and more honest to just pay people enough to live on?

    Email
    Tweet
  • Why should I subsidize rich employers like McDonalds or Walmart?

    Food stamps are a pillar of these employers' business model. They pay too little, knowing that the taxpayers will pay the rest.

    Email
    Tweet
  • If full-time workers qualify for food stamps, the minimum wage is too low.

    A poverty wage is when full-time workers need assistance from the government – like food stamps, Medicaid, and housing benefits – to make ends meet.

    Email
    Tweet
  • The purpose of the minimum wage is to prevent exploitation.

    In this sense, minimum wage laws are like workplace safety regulations.

    Email
    Tweet
  • Yes, it's designed to kill jobs – sweatshop jobs.

    If your business can only thrive by exploiting workers, then you shouldn't be in business.

    Email
    Tweet
  • Raising the minimum wage will actually boost the economy.

    Every extra dollar that low-income workers earn is spent. Basic economics tells us that more spending means more jobs, with ripple effects across the economy.

Reasons Why Not

  • They don't deserve it.

    Not every worker is worth the same amount. It's crazy to tell business owners that every retail clerk or burger flipper is worth, say, $15 per hour.

    Email
    Tweet
  • What about the folks who currently earn, say, $15?

    Raising the minimum wage will be unfair to skilled workers who will be earning no more than unskilled workers.

    Email
    Tweet
  • It will cause inflation.

    Raising the minimum wage hurts middle-class people, because it will mean higher prices for things that are already expensive, like food and clothing.

    Email
    Tweet
  • Raising the minimum wage will kill jobs.

    Small businesses are America's real job creators. And when small business owners are forced to pay higher wages, that forces them to lay off workers or stop hiring.

    Email
    Tweet
  • Let the Market set the wage.

    Wages go up or down with the economy. Politicians should be working on getting the economy in better shape instead of burdening businesses with higher costs.
